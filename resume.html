Ben Ayers-Glassey


I'm a self-taught programmer. After getting a math degree on the East coast, I worked my way across Canada, and have begun a career in software development on the West coast.

In my spare time, I make games and small programming languages, pushing the boundaries of my abilities and imagination.

At work, I try to write boring, solid code, so that it doesn't break on production over the weekend.

To that end, I:

  • talk with other people whose code will interface with mine
  • spend time with management and clients hashing out requirements
  • write code in manageable units with small APIs, a bit of documentation, and a couple of tests
I'm interested in working with people who share my passion, which is: to create excellent software.

A huge bonus for me would be spare time to study new technologies and work on my own projects.
